§ 01 / Practice

My work sits at three intersections. First, representation: how do you encode a molecule, a peptide, an antibody, a protein, so that a model trained on one modality can reason about another? I work on graph neural networks for small molecules, transformer language models for sequences (ChemBERTa, ESM-2, ABLang-2), and the fusion architectures that let them share evidence.

Second, reliability: ML models in drug discovery are wrong constantly. The question is whether they know they’re wrong. I build out-of-distribution detection, conformal prediction, and assay-quality-aware uncertainty into the property prediction platform my team ships.

Third, leverage: a research org of 30,000 people benefits more from durable tooling than from one-off model wins. I build, package, and distribute internal AI infrastructure — skills, libraries, reference architectures — that other scientists pick up and use.
