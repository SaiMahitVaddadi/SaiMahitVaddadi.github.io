02 — STRATEGY

How I engage.

Not every business needs AI. Some need process clarity first. When AI does apply, the right scope matters more than the right algorithm. Here is how I figure out which is which.

01

Audit

Map the territory.

The first step is always the same: understand your workflows, your data, and where the real friction is. This is a working session — I am looking for the gap between what you have and what AI could do, not selling you on a predetermined solution.

Output: a prioritized list of AI opportunities, ranked by impact and feasibility, with a clear implementation roadmap. You leave with something actionable whether or not we work together further.

Duration 1–2 days
Format Working sessions + written report
Output Prioritized roadmap + feasibility assessment

02

Build

Build it.

Design and implementation of the two or three AI integrations that move the needle most. This is hands-on work: I write the code, configure the infrastructure, and deploy it in your environment.

Typical builds: document processing pipelines, customer-facing assistants, internal knowledge tools, automation workflows, and data extraction from unstructured sources.

Duration 2–8 weeks depending on scope
Format Iterative with weekly check-ins
Output Deployed, working system with documentation

03

Equip

Equip your team.

A system that only works when I am there is not a system. The equip phase transfers knowledge: your team learns to maintain, extend, and iterate on what we built together.

This includes documentation, training sessions, and a 30-day async support window after handoff. The goal is zero ongoing dependency on me.

Duration 1 week + 30-day async support
Format Training sessions + documentation
Output Self-sufficient team, documented system
